BAGS: Blur Agnostic Gaussian Splatting through Multi-Scale Kernel Modeling

Cheng Peng1*, Yutao Tang1*, Yifan Zhou1, Nengyu Wang1, Xijun Liu1, Deming Li1, Rama Chellappa1
1Johns Hopkins University *Equal contribution
Arxiv Paper Supple Code Data

BAGS reconstructs scenes in high quality, and remove various image degradation.

Abstract

Recent efforts in using 3D Gaussian for scene reconstruction and novel view synthesis can achieve impressive results on curated benchmarks; however, images captured in real life are often noisy. In this work, we analyze the robustness of Gaussian-Splatting-based methods against various image degradation, such as motion blur, defocus blur, low resolution , etc. Under these scenarios, Gaussian-Splatting-based methods overfit to degradation and produce worse results than Neural-Radiance-Field-based methods. To address this issue, we propose Robust Gaussian Splatting (Robust-GS). Robust-GS introduces additional 2D modeling capacities such that a 3D-consistent and high quality scene can be reconstructed despite image-wise artifacts. Specifically, we model image degradation by estimating per-pixel convolution kernels from a Degradation Proposal Network (DPN). DPN is designed to consider spatial, color, and depth variations of the scene to maximize modeling capacity. Additionally, DPN also proposes a quality-assessing mask, which indicates observed regions where degradation occurs. Finally, we introduce a coarse-to-fine kernel optimization scheme; this optimization scheme is fast and avoids sub-optimal solutions due to sparse point cloud initialization, which often occurs when we apply Structure-from-Motion from noisy images. We demonstrate that Robust-GS achieves much more photorealistic renderings under various challenging degradation conditions and significantly improves upon existing approaches.

Pipeline

BAGS is optimized by training a Blur Proposal Network on top of the scene over multiple scales. We extract features from color and depth, which are concatenated with the position and view embedding to form the Multi-Modal Feature (MMF). The kernel MLP then estimates the per-pixel kernel and mask. We use the kernel to model the blur image and employ the mask to blend the rendered image and the blur-modeled image, yielding the output image. After multiple steps, we upscale image resolution and modify the kernel MLP to produce a kernel with a larger kernel size.
